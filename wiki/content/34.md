# Animations

The **Animations** tab (in the Mesh Editor's Auto Rig side panel, next to **[Skeleton](33.md)**) lets you retarget ready-made animation clips onto your rigged mesh, preview them live, and save the mesh with the animations you like embedded.

Animations are **retargeted** from a *reference mesh* — a pre-animated character whose motion is transferred onto your mesh's skeleton. To do that, the reference skeleton's bones must first be mapped to your mesh's bones (see **[Bone Mapping](35.md)**).

> This tab only works on a **rigged** mesh. Run **[Auto Rig](32.md)** first (or load a mesh that already has a skeleton).

## Workflow

1. **Select a reference mesh** from the dropdown. This is the source of the animation library.
2. Click **Map bones** to open the **[Bone Mapping](35.md)** dialog and match the reference skeleton to your mesh. (Once mapped, this button becomes **Edit mapping**.)
3. The animation clips from that reference load into the list. **Click a clip** to retarget it onto your mesh and preview it playing in the viewport.
4. **Tick** the clips you want to keep.
5. Click **Save mesh with N animations** to embed the selected clips and save the mesh as a **new version** in your library.

## Playback options

Once a mapping exists, extra controls appear above the clip list:

| Control | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Auto-align to floor** | Sit the animated mesh on the floor grid so it doesn't sink or float during playback. |
| **Expand / Contract arms** | A slider (−100% → +100%) that spreads or tucks the arms. Useful when the retargeted rest pose has arms too wide or too close to the body. A reset button returns it to 0. *(Shown only when the mesh's rig supports arm adjustment.)* |

## Searching and selecting clips

- The **search box** filters the clip list by name. The header shows the filtered count (e.g. `12 / 80` when a search is active).
- Each clip has a **preview thumbnail** and a **checkbox**. Clicking a clip selects it for live preview; ticking its box marks it to be embedded on save.
- The **Save** button is disabled until at least one clip is ticked, and shows a count of how many will be embedded.

## Saving

**Save mesh with N animations** embeds the ticked clips into the mesh and stores it as a **new version** of the asset. The original (unanimated) version is left untouched, so you can always go back.
