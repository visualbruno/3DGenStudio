# Skeleton

When the current mesh is rigged (after running **[Auto Rig](32.md)**, or when you load an already-rigged mesh), a panel appears on the right of the Mesh Editor with two tabs: **Skeleton** and **[Animations](34.md)**. This page covers the **Skeleton** tab.

## Bone hierarchy

The Skeleton tab shows every bone in the mesh as a collapsible **hierarchy tree**, rooted at the skeleton's root bone(s). The header reports the total bone count.

- Bones with children show a **chevron** — click it to expand or collapse that branch.
- Each row is labelled with the bone's name (using whatever naming convention you chose in Auto Rig — Mixamo, UE5, or the model's own names).

## Selecting bones

Bone selection is **two-way** with the viewport:

- **Click a bone row** in the panel to select it. The matching bone is highlighted on the mesh via the skeleton overlay.
- **Click a bone on the mesh** to select it here. Its row is highlighted, its collapsed ancestors are automatically expanded so it is revealed, and the tree scrolls it into view.

This makes it easy to confirm *which* physical bone a name refers to — names alone rarely tell you where a bone actually sits.

## Requirements

The Skeleton panel only appears while you are in **Auto Rig** mode and the current mesh actually has a skeleton. If the mesh has no bones, the panel shows an empty state instead of a tree.

---

To animate a rigged mesh, switch to the **[Animations](34.md)** tab.
